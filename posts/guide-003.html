## 슬라이드 7: "System Interconnects" — 이게 뭐라고?

Okay, so everyone’s been buzzing about the slides showing PRISM’s *capabilities*, right? Like, how they grab data from all these tech giants. But nobody seems to be talking about the one labeled `Slide 7: System Interconnects`. It looks like a pretty dry system diagram at first glance, a bunch of boxes and lines. But if you zoom in, and I mean *really* zoom in, you start seeing some… interesting stuff.

It’s not just about *what* data is being accessed, but *how* the system is architected to do it. There’s mention of specific protocols, like `[protocol_variant: Secure_Transport_Layer_v1.0_extended]` and `[data_exchange_format: XML_v2.3_strict]`. This isn't the kind of detail you find in your average news report. They’re talking about how different internal nodes within the NSA infrastructure communicate with each other to process the incoming streams.

### 데이터 흐름 분석: 단순 감시 이상의 것

What really caught my eye was the section detailing the `[node_type: Data_Aggregation_Hub_3B]` and its interaction with what they call `[processing_unit_model: Quantum_Resonance_Processor_X1]`. Now, I’m no quantum physicist, but the fact that they’re talking about a specific hardware model, and mentioning something about "real-time resonance synchronization," makes me think this is way beyond just simple data collection. It suggests a level of sophisticated processing that’s… frankly, a bit mind-boggling.

I saw a spike in Google Trends for `[obscure_keyword_1: 'quantum resonance processor']` around the time the initial leaks happened, but it was tiny, like a blip. And then, a few weeks *later*, there was another, slightly bigger spike for `[obscure_keyword_2: 'NSA node communication protocols']`. See that? There's a definite *lag* between the actual tech detail being documented and people even starting to look for it online. Most folks just see the big picture, the headlines.
